## The Brief

The clients — a couple in their forties, both architects by training — had bought the top two floors of a new tower in Koramangala in shell-and-core condition. They wanted a home that read as one apartment, not two stacked ones, and that could hold a small library, a piano, and a quiet place to draw.

## The Approach

We removed the developer&rsquo;s standard staircase and replaced it with a single full-height void at the centre of the plan. A new oak stair rises through it, lit from a clerestory cut into the upper slab. The plan radiates outward from this stair: living and dining on one side, study and library on the other.

## Materials & Detail

Floors throughout are wide-plank fumed oak. Walls are lime-washed in a single warm white that shifts perceptibly through the day. Joinery is in oxidised brass and figured walnut. The piano sits on a small inset of black kota stone — a quiet plinth.

## Outcome

The apartment reads as a single, slow loft. The library has filled. The piano gets played in the afternoons.

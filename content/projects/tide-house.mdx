## The Brief

A weekend house in north Goa for a family of three, used twelve weekends a year. The brief was small: a kitchen worth cooking in, three bedrooms, a covered verandah long enough to sleep on, and as little upkeep as possible.

## The Approach

The plan is a single-storey L wrapped around an old mango tree the clients refused to cut. The verandah forms the inner edge of the L and runs the full length of the house. Every habitable room opens onto it.

## Materials & Detail

Walls are lime-washed in pale ochre. Roof is in handmade Mangalore tile on exposed rafters. Floors throughout are oxide red — laid by a craftsman from Karwar across two slow weeks. Windows are local jackwood with brass espagnolettes.

## Outcome

The verandah gets used more than any room. The mango tree is fine.

## The Brief

The owners — a couple returning to India after fifteen years in hospitality abroad — wanted a hotel that didn&rsquo;t feel like a hotel. Twenty-two rooms, no banquet hall, one restaurant, and a single library open to guests around the clock.

## The Approach

The plan organises every room around a long, narrow central courtyard planted with a single neem tree. Corridors are deep enough to walk slowly; rooms are smaller than the brief suggested but with full-height window seats that look down into the courtyard or out into a tangle of jacaranda.

## Materials & Detail

Floors are honed black kota in the public areas, broad-plank teak in the rooms. Bathrooms are clad in handmade Athangudi tile, sourced from a single workshop in Tamil Nadu. Lighting is warm, low, and indirect — there is no overhead fixture in any guest room.

## Outcome

The hotel has a steady eighty-percent occupancy and the kind of repeat-stay rate that a banquet hall would not have produced.

## The Brief

A consultancy of forty had outgrown a glass-walled floor that none of them liked. They wanted a workplace that read as a single room — not a chain of meeting cubes — and that could hold long working hours without feeling clinical.

## The Approach

We took the entire floor down to slab and re-planned it around two long oak benches that run the length of the space. Meeting rooms are pushed to the periphery and skinned in fluted glass; the centre stays open. A small library and a tea kitchen sit at one end, on a slightly raised plinth.

## Materials & Detail

Acoustic plaster ceiling throughout. Floors in raw concrete, sealed and waxed. Workbenches in solid oak, oversized and shared. Task lighting is per-seat — there are no overhead fluorescents on the working floor.

## Outcome

The team reports they stay later but with less fatigue. The library has acquired a habit of lunchtime reading.

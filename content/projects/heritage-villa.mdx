## The Brief

The clients had grown up next door to this 1920s bungalow and bought it after years of watching it fall slowly out of use. They wanted to keep what was theirs to inherit — the courtyards, the Madras terrace, the teak rafters — and to add only what a young family of four would actually use day to day.

## The Approach

We spent the first three weeks on site rather than on the drawing board. We mapped every load-bearing wall, every original opening, and the grain of light through each room across a single day. The plan that emerged kept the entire historic envelope intact and grafted a small, low addition at the rear — a kitchen and a study that open onto a re-cut courtyard.

Where original lime plaster had survived, we restored it. Where it hadn&rsquo;t, we matched it by hand with a craftsperson who works almost exclusively on heritage homes in the city.

## Materials & Detail

Reclaimed teak from the original roof was reused as a single dining table and as new internal door frames. The terrazzo floor in the front rooms was patched, polished, and left otherwise unchanged. New joinery is in mango wood with brushed brass fittings, made by a workshop in Bommanahalli we have worked with since 2020.

## Outcome

The house now houses three generations and a dog. The street facade is unchanged from the day it was built. From the rear courtyard, a quiet contemporary house sits behind it.
